Medavize Brand Guidelines
1. Brand Essence
Medavize is a health-tech platform designed as a "companion app" for caregivers. The brand bridges the gap between complex medical data and the human experience of caregiving, focusing on simplifying the journey to wellness through technology.
	•	Mission: To lighten the burden of caregiving, simplify care, and equip families with insights to provide better support.
	•	Core Pillars: * Personalized: Tailored to the unique needs of the caregiver and patient.
	◦	Simple: Removing technical and medical friction.
	◦	Secure: Ensuring HIPAA-level data integrity and trust.

2. Visual Identity
Logo & Iconography
	•	The Mark: The brand utilizes clean, modern medical and technological iconography (e.g., the HIPAA shield and stylized human-centric icons).
	•	Philosophy: Minimalist designs that prioritize clarity over complexity.
Color Palette
Medavize uses a "Cool & Trustworthy" palette, typical of high-end health-tech, to evoke calmness and professionalism.
	•	Primary Navy/Deep Blue: Represents authority, medical expertise, and security.
	•	Teal/Soft Green: Represents healing, growth, and the "wellness" aspect of the brand.
	•	White/Neutral Gray: Used for "breathability" and clean UI, reflecting a stress-free user experience.
Typography
	•	Headlines: Bold, clean sans-serif (reminiscent of modern fonts like Montserrat or Inter) to convey strength and modernity.
	•	Body Text: Highly legible sans-serif with generous line spacing to ensure accessibility for caregivers who may be under stress or in low-light environments.

3. Imagery & Photography
Medavize moves away from clinical, cold medical imagery in favor of Lifestyle Caregiving.
	•	Subject Matter: Multi-generational interactions (e.g., a daughter helping her father, a son assisting a mother with therapy).
	•	Tone: Warm, natural lighting with a focus on connection and support rather than just the "illness."
	•	Authenticity: Real-world scenarios that reflect the 27+ hours a week caregivers spend on care.

4. Voice and Tone

5. Brand Positioning
Medavize positions itself as a "Companion," not just a tool. It sits at the intersection of:
	1	Caregiver Support: Recognizing the sacrifice of time and energy.
	2	Medical Intelligence: Providing insights for doctor visits and new diagnoses.
	3	Preventative Care: Moving from "reactive" to "proactive" management.

6. Digital Presence & Social
	•	Platforms: Active on LinkedIn, Instagram, Facebook, and YouTube.
	•	Content Strategy: Sharing data-driven insights (e.g., the "59 million caregivers" statistic) paired with personal stories of wellness and simplicity.

7. Landing Page UI Patterns & Implementation
This section documents the landing page design system as working instructions for designers, frontend developers, and LLM agents.

7.1 Color Scheme
•	Header/Footer: Black (#000000) - creates high contrast and premium feel
•	Background Colors:
	- Hero Section: Neutral-100 (#f5f5f4) - darker gray for contrast
	- Feature Cards Section: White (#ffffff) - clean card background
	- CTA Section: Neutral-100 (#f5f5f4) - matches hero for visual balance
•	Accent Color: Emerald (#00A86B) - primary action color, represents health/growth
•	Text Colors:
	- Primary: Foreground (#0d1b2a) - dark for readability
	- Secondary: Muted (#6b7c93) - for supporting text
	- Links: Emerald-600 (#00A86B) - interactive elements

7.2 Layout Structure
•	Single-column mobile-first layout
•	Section separation through alternating backgrounds (neutral-100/white) with border separators
•	Vertical stack pattern with consistent spacing (py-10 for sections)
•	Full-width elements on mobile, centered content with max-width on larger screens

7.4 Design Patterns for LLM Agents

7.4.1 Section Separation Pattern
•	Use alternating backgrounds (neutral-100 / white) to create visual rhythm
•	Add border-b border-neutral-200 between sections for clear separation
•	Consistent vertical spacing: py-10 for section containers
•	Pattern: Dark → Light → Dark creates visual hierarchy

7.4.2 Card Pattern
•	Cards should be compact by default (icon + title only)
•	Use expand/collapse pattern for content-heavy cards
•	"More/Less" button with chevron icon indicates expandable state
•	Icon background color should match accent color (emerald-50)
•	Shadow should be subtle (shadow-card)

7.4.3 Button Pattern
•	Primary actions: Full-width, pill-shaped (rounded-full), emerald background
•	Secondary actions: Text links with emerald color
•	Consistent padding: py-4 for primary buttons
•	Shadow on primary buttons for depth (shadow-lg)

7.4.4 Color Usage Rules
•	Black for header/footer - premium feel, high contrast
•	White for card sections - clean, breathable
•	Neutral-100 for hero/CTA - subtle contrast without harshness
•	Emerald (#00A86B) for all interactive elements and accents
•	Muted-foreground for supporting text

7.5 Implementation Notes for Developers
•	Tech stack: React + TypeScript + Tailwind CSS
•	Icons: lucide-react library
•	Responsive: Mobile-first design, single column on mobile
•	State management: useState for card expand/collapse
•	Logo file: logo-white.png in /public folder
•	Tailwind config uses custom colors: emerald, neutral, foreground, muted-foreground
•	Border radius: rounded-xl (12px) for cards, rounded-full for buttons
•	Shadows: shadow-card (subtle), shadow-lg (elevated)

7.6 Accessibility Guidelines
•	Contrast ratios: Black/white meets WCAG AAA, emerald/white meets WCAG AA
•	Tap targets: Minimum 44px height for interactive elements
•	Font sizes: Minimum 14px (text-sm) for body text, 28px for headlines
•	Line spacing: leading-relaxed for better readability
•	Focus states: Hover states on all interactive elements
